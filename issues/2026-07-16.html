The Lead
Apple can finally sell AI in China — just not its own
Apple shares jumped 4% to a record $327 on Wednesday after China's Cyberspace Administration approved Apple Intelligence for the domestic market — clearing a filing process that had stalled the feature since the iPhone 16 launched in September 2024. The twist: Apple can't use its own US-trained models under Chinese law, so Alibaba's Qwen will power the text and image generation, with Baidu also in the mix. The timing helps — iPhone shipments in China were already up 24.4% year-on-year in Q2 — and a working AI assistant should extend that run. No launch date is confirmed yet.

Why it matters: In China, Apple doesn't get to build the AI, it has to rent it from a local rival — and that dependency is now the template every foreign phone maker will have to follow.
The Takeaway
Apple's stock didn't move on a new feature — it moved on getting permission to ship an old one. Regulatory approval is now a bigger swing factor for hardware stocks than the AI itself, and every company selling into China should plan around renting the model, not owning it.

🤖 AI & Tech
The frontier labs are all building the same second business
Anthropic launched a $1.5 billion implementation venture called Ode with Blackstone, Goldman Sachs and Hellman & Friedman — 100 engineers deployed to help enterprises actually use Claude, not just license it. OpenAI is running the identical playbook through its own Deployment Company. The read: models are commoditizing, and the money is in the plumbing.

Google pushed Gemini 3.5 Pro to July 17, per unconfirmed third-party reporting, after scrapping its original architecture for a ground-up rebuild — the second slip since May's I/O reveal. Neither the rumoured 2-million-token context window nor pricing has been confirmed by Google.

Meta is reportedly building a GPU-rental business, Meta Compute, letting customers rent spare capacity or run models on its infrastructure — a direct shot at AWS, Azure and Google Cloud. The stock popped as much as 10% on the report; CoreWeave and Nebius sold off on the threat of a much bigger landlord entering their market.

Samsung and SK Hynix committed $880 billion to AI chips and data centres over ten years, with Seoul orchestrating rather than funding the build-out. Four new fabs are planned, and the timeline has been pulled forward roughly a decade — a sign of how fast the AI capex race is compressing planning horizons everywhere.

Chamath Palihapitiya took the CEO seat at 8090 Labs, which raised $135 million led by Salesforce Ventures for "Software Factory" — an agentic coding platform for healthcare, finance and government clients, where compliance review is the real bottleneck, not writing code. His first operating role since leaving Facebook in 2011.

📈 Markets
A denial didn't stop Lucid from losing half its value
The S&P 500 closed Wednesday at 7,572.40 (+0.38%) and the Nasdaq Composite at 26,269.23 (+0.62%), both lifted as Apple hit an all-time high and Amazon, Alphabet and Microsoft each gained roughly 3% on cooling-inflation optimism.

Lucid called the report "completely false" and says it has liquidity into next year — but the stock still closed down double digits. A denial doesn't un-ring a 55% bell.

💡 Product Craft
Steal this growth tactic: sell your audience's attention twice
Lenny Rachitsky's newsletter runs one of the best growth mechanics in software right now: Lenny's Product Pass bundles a free year of 23-plus premium tools — Canva, ElevenLabs, Linear, Railway, Manus — worth over $25,000, exclusively for annual subscribers. Neither side pays cash. Lenny gets the highest-leverage conversion in any subscription business, monthly-to-annual; each partner gets a shot at 18,000-plus high-intent product builders who'll actually use a B2B tool, at zero CAC.

The mechanic isn't new — cable channels bundled decades ago — but stacking it with renewable software subscriptions turns a newsletter into a distribution network. If you sell to the same audience as someone else's newsletter, the co-marketing budget you're looking for might already exist as their subscriber list.
