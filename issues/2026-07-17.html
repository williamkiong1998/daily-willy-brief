The Lead
Alphabet's Gemini delay just became a stock-price problem
Alphabet shares sank as much as 4% on Thursday after Bloomberg reported that Gemini 3.5 Pro — promised for June at Google's I/O keynote — is months late because its coding performance fell short of internal targets. The real story is organisational: DeepMind, Cloud, Android and Search are all building overlapping AI coding tools, and the duplication is slowing everyone down instead of speeding anyone up. The timing stings, too — both OpenAI and Meta have shipped newer models that already out-code Google's current lineup. No new release date has been confirmed.

Why it matters: In a race sold on speed, Google just showed that the most compute doesn't guarantee you ship first — coordination is now the harder problem than compute.
The Takeaway
Model quality isn't the bottleneck for any frontier lab anymore — shipping discipline is. And the same week Google stumbled on execution, Microsoft, Anthropic and OpenAI all doubled down on services businesses built to solve exactly that problem for everyone else's AI rollouts too.

🤖 AI & Tech
Every AI giant is now selling the same second business: showing up
Microsoft launched a $2.5 billion "Frontier Company", embedding 6,000 engineers directly inside enterprise customers to co-design and deploy AI systems — following Anthropic's Ode and OpenAI's Deployment Company into the same trade. The reason all three are chasing it: roughly 95% of enterprise AI pilots reportedly deliver zero measurable ROI. Selling the model was always the easy part.

Nvidia is pitching its CPUs as a $200 billion opportunity it hasn't touched yet. Jensen Huang told reporters in Tokyo the Vera CPU line has "visibility to almost $20 billion" in revenue this year alone. Nvidia also unveiled a new physical-AI model for robots — a hedge in case GPU demand ever plateaus.

Apple sued OpenAI over alleged trade-secret theft, accusing two former Apple hardware engineers of downloading confidential files before leaving for the ChatGPT maker. OpenAI says it has seen no evidence backing the claim. The timing is pointed — OpenAI's own first hardware device is reportedly close to shipping.

An AI coding startup became a unicorn in a year: Emergent raised a $130 million Series C at a $1.5 billion valuation, five times January's mark, led by Creaegis. It's now past 200,000 paying customers and $120 million in annualised revenue, up 70% in four months — targeting production software, not prototypes.

OpenAI's first hardware bet is a speaker with no screen, built with Jony Ive's team for a reported 2027 launch. The pitch isn't utility — Alexa already does timers — it's "personality," with mechanical elements designed to make the device feel alive. Selling a relationship, not a feature.

📈 Markets
Record profit, falling stock: TSMC's guidance spooked the whole chip trade
The S&P 500 closed Thursday at 7,533.77, down 0.51%, and the Nasdaq Composite fell 1.47% to 25,881.95, giving back two days of gains in a single session as a chip selloff spread well beyond the stock that caused it.

TSMC posted its fifth consecutive quarter of record profit and beat estimates on revenue and margins — then watched its US shares slide 1.7% anyway, because it raised 2026 capex guidance to $60-64 billion, up from $52-56 billion, alongside a $100 billion Arizona build-out. Investors read record profit plus rising spend as shrinking free cash flow, and dragged Micron, SK Hynix and the rest of the memory trade down with it.

The sharp number: Nvidia, still holding a $5.1 trillion market cap at $205.70 a share, is now pitching its Vera CPU line as a fresh $200 billion opportunity it hasn't started billing for yet. Expect earnings season — Netflix and UnitedHealth report Friday — to keep AI capex commentary, not headline sales, as the number that actually moves stocks.
💼 Business Story
A pension fund, four blue-chip co-investors, one very convincing lie
Malaysia's Finance Ministry confirmed this week that KWAP, the retirement fund for civil servants, was defrauded out of RM200 million (US$47.7 million) it invested in Indonesian agritech unicorn eFishery back in 2023. KWAP wasn't reckless — it invested alongside Temasek, SoftBank, 42XFund and Northstar, each running its own due diligence. All of them missed it: eFishery's founders had inflated revenue by roughly $600 million and turned a real $35 million loss into a reported $16 million profit. Co-founder Gibran Huzaifah is now serving nine years in an Indonesian prison; recovery efforts are underway but far from guaranteed.

The lesson for anyone syndicating a check: a crowded cap table of famous names isn't verification, it's just consensus — and consensus can be wrong together as easily as alone.
