THE WILLY BRIEF
Tuesday, 7 July 2026 · Kuala Lumpur
SpaceX went public three weeks ago; today every index fund on the planet is forced to own a piece of it, whether the price makes sense to them or not. Add a Chinese military purge reaching two new generals, a NATO summit trying to collect on last year's promises, and early voting in Johor, and Tuesday is shaping up to be a lesson in things moving faster than anyone gets to vote on.
📌 The Lead
SpaceX joins the Nasdaq-100 before today's open — 15 trading days after its record $75 billion IPO on June 12, the fastest any company has entered a major US index. It's only possible because Nasdaq rewrote its own inclusion rules on May 1, letting any newly listed top-40-by-market-cap stock skip the usual waiting period entirely. The mechanical effect: every fund tracking the index, roughly $800 billion in assets, must now buy SPCX regardless of price conviction — an estimated $4.3 billion of forced buying lands today alone. Not everyone's convinced: the stock has already slid 28% from its post-debut high of $225 to around $162. The IPO's quiet period also lifts today, finally letting bank analysts publish independent verdicts on whether $1.75 trillion was ever the right number.
Why it matters: A rule change, not a fundamental, just moved billions in ETF money overnight — index membership is now a growth lever you can engineer for, not a reward for surviving long enough to earn it.
🤖 AI & Tech
Gemini 3.5 Pro is rolling into Vertex AI's enterprise preview with a 2-million-token context window — the largest of any production frontier model — though Google has yet to set a firm general-availability date. For builders drowning users in retrieval pipelines to fake long memory, that's a real shortcut, if quality holds at scale.
GLM-5.2 reignited the "is China catching up" argument. Z.ai's open-weight model, trained on roughly 100,000 Huawei Ascend chips, now tops open-model leaderboards and trails Claude Opus 4.8 by low single digits on coding benchmarks — for free, under an MIT license. Export controls were supposed to be a capability ceiling; that theory just took a hit.
Meta quietly shipped Pocket, an app that turns text prompts into playable mini-games with no code — "make a drawing game where the flower is the paintbrush" is a real example prompt. It's Meta's clearest bet yet that vibe-coding becomes a mainstream habit, not just a developer one.
Washington is racing to finish AI release rules before frontier labs ship again: OpenAI and Anthropic have reportedly agreed to limit early access to their newest models to government-vetted customers while a 30-day security review framework gets finalised with the White House.
SK Hynix is testing the market's nerve with a $29 billion Nasdaq stock offering this week — a bet that appetite for AI memory-chip exposure hasn't cooled, even with the sector still climbing out of June's selloff.

📈 Markets
US stocks closed at fresh records Monday: the S&P 500 gained 0.72% to 7,537.43, the Nasdaq Composite rose 1.12% to 26,121.16, and the Dow crossed 53,000 for the first time. The day's real story was semiconductors clawing back June's losses — AMD jumped 6.6%, its best day since the selloff, as investors re-priced Q1 data-center revenue of $5.8 billion, up 57% year-over-year, against AI compute demand that keeps outrunning supply. Broadcom added 3.7% on an extended Apple partnership, and Apple is up more than 12% in five sessions on reports of five new iPhone models, including a $2,500 foldable. Sharp number: SK Hynix is pricing a $29 billion Nasdaq offering this week, the market's next referendum on AI memory-chip appetite. Expect volatility into Q2 earnings season (Apple reports July 30) and as today's SpaceX quiet-period expiry unleashes the first independent analyst notes on the year's biggest listing.
🧠 Product Craft
The growth playbook doesn't survive contact with AI products. Elena Verna spent 15 years building growth at SurveyMonkey, Miro and Amplitude; she now runs growth at Lovable, the AI app-builder that hit $200 million ARR in under a year. Her verdict, from a recent conversation with Lenny Rachitsky: 60-70% of what she knew stopped applying. Activation isn't one "aha" moment anymore — it's proving output quality across dozens of generations before a user trusts the tool with real work. And pricing can't stay seat-based when your marginal cost is a GPU call, not a login. The lesson for anyone bolting AI features onto an existing product: audit which of your growth assumptions were actually about software, and which were quietly about the human labor you've now automated away.
🎯 The Closer
SpaceX debuted three weeks ago at a $1.75 trillion valuation. It's down 28% from its post-IPO high, and today it joins the Nasdaq-100 by rule change rather than by merit. Being forced into every index fund on Earth doesn't stop the market from marking you down anyway — scale bought inclusion, not conviction.
